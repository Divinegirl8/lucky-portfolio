    • Overview

    Bosscab is a ride-hailing app which offers transportation services for private drivers and riders between two or more locations. Each client is different, which makes every trip unique. However, this diversity is hard to identify for drivers prior to meeting the passenger.

    Bosscab is vocal about its mission to be the friendlier, fairer option in the rideshare space. In anticipation of going public, Bosscab needed to show continued growth by attracting new riders and drivers. Increasing both at the same time would mean more rides, shorter wait times, and higher revenue.

    The goal for this project was to improve the app information architecture, and overall rider experience in terms of smooth payment,reduced cost of ride, sorting out wallet transfer, and ride security.

    I redesigned Bosscab with these values and goals in mind, inspiring more people to sign up or apply to drive.

    The Challenge

    How might we make Bosscab rides more enjoyable emphatic and affordable?

    01.

    Discover

    Problem Exploration & User Research

    USER INTERVIEWS

    I learned from certain data that 80% of riders complain of price surge from ride hailing companies. There is a certain ride hailing company that came up with the idea of users choosing a price they can afford but research shows that drivers don’t accept the rides most times because it doesn’t favor their income considering prices of gasoline and petrol, and traffic too.

    Also, there is the issue of delayed transaction or transaction failure between drivers and riders.

    In order to empathize with users, I conducted user interviews with 10 participants. I wanted to understand their real experiences while using other ride hailing services and see how we can improve on Bosscab features to serve the users in a truly creative fashion. I created an interview guide, which listed open-ended questions, that helped me facilitate the interview.

    “Ride hailing apps are becoming so expensive.”

    “Sometimes I get debited twice when I pay for a ride.”

    “Drivers turn down my request when they see the amount I am willing to pay.”

    “Late arrival to my destination.”

    “Drivers arrive late sometimes.”

    “Delay in transaction processes.”

    02.

    Define

    Identifying User Pain Points

    SYNTHESISING RESEARCH, PRODUCT REQUIREMENTS, HMWs, USER STORIES, AFFINITY MAPPING, USER PERSONA

    To analyze the qualitative data that I gathered through user interviews, I used an affinity map to find common themes among the ride hailing users’ feedback. I divided it up into three sections to see how users behave, how they respond, and how they use other ride app features.

    Focusing on what seemed the most feasible, I identified three pain points of the users’ experiences: lack of affordability, clunky payment process, and desynchronized map reading. I created a user persona to embody the key audience that would benefit the most from this new feature: a social, young professional in a large city. Creating a user persona helped to tackle the various pain points and needs generated from user research into a single "person" that I can easily reference during the design process.

    03.

    Develop

    Brainstorming Solutions

    CONCEPT EXPLORATION, MIND MAPPING, COMPETITIVE ANALYSIS

    Staying one step ahead of competitors usually results in success. To do so, I investigated different ways to create an advantage for Bosscab. I know Bosscab is entering a crowded market, so I made strategic decisions to differentiate the product as the obvious choice for consumers.

    I brainstormed solutions by creating a mind map that laid out potential solutions to the user persona’s pain points. To guide me in creating more solutions, I completed a competitive analysis to see how other ride-hailing apps solved similar issues and to see if any of my ideas had already been implemented. I chose to move forward with the idea of having an IN-APP WALLET to avoid transaction delay after rides, and a GROUP WALLET so that friends and corporate bodies can come together, create a group wallet on Bosscab, fund it, and pay for rides from the wallet. Group wallet will emphatically help riders who don’t have much money to keep up with ride hailing. Companies and government agencies can fund a group wallet for their staffs as part of incentives for working with them. About desynched map direction, I came up with the idea of BOSSCAB MAP LOCALIZATION that would fit into the local settings of any city, direct drivers properly and ensure early arrivals. Since this proposed features would tackle all three pain points, it was the most well-rounded and practical solution.

    Structuring The Experience

    TASK FLOW, USER FLOW, SKETCHES

    Once I decided on my idea of adding a “Group Wallet” feature, I created a task flow to map out the steps the user will take to utilize this new feature. This was challenging as a new feature must not feel out of place in the current flow, and creating a framework was important for sketching out the screens. Stepping into the persona's shoes, I created a user flow that mapped out the decision-making points to ensure that the flow is intuitive.

    04.

    Deliver

    Prototyping, Testing, and Refining the Solution

    HI-FI PROTOTYPE, HI-FI DESIGN, USABILITY TESTING

    Using my sketches, I created a hi-fi prototype to test the new “Group Wallet” feature using Bosscab’s existing style guidelines. My main goal was to validate the potential demand for this new feature as well as quickly assess and uncover any usability issues I may have overlooked. I gathered 10 participants and utilized Maze, a rapid prototyping tool, and discovered a few usability issues within the prototype. I then brainstormed solutions and made several adjustments to directly address user’s feedback.

    Solving Usability Bugs

    USABILITY TESTING

    I conducted a second usability test with 10 new participants and found that the designs directly addressed the usability issues uncovered from the first test. I found there was a large improvement in several usability metrics, including misclick rate, average duration, and bounce rate. The users were able to complete the tasks more quickly, with a higher success rate.
